Benchmark Recommendation Summary
🛡️
Downside Protection
Nifty 50 absorbs shocks better — shallower drawdowns in 60.5% of all periods
Max drawdown of −59.5% vs −63.7% for Nifty 500. Calmar ratio 0.237 vs 0.225. Sortino 0.446 vs 0.441. Nifty 50 consistently loses less in bad markets.
Max DD: −59.5% vs −63.7% Calmar: 0.237 vs 0.225 Sortino: 0.446 vs 0.441
⏱️
Recovery Speed
Nifty 50 rebounds nearly 3× faster after market crashes
GFC recovery: 704 days for Nifty 50 vs 1,977 days for Nifty 500. Avg trough-to-peak 64 days vs 89 days. Quicker recovery means less time underwater for investors.
GFC Recovery: 704 vs 1,977 days Avg T2P: 64 vs 89 days 2.8× faster rebound
📈
Upside Capture
Nifty 50 captures 107% of Nifty 500 rallies — more upside, less downside
Upside beta of 1.073 vs downside beta of 0.977 creates a favourable asymmetry. Nifty 50 participates more in up markets and falls less in down markets — the ideal benchmark characteristic.
Upside Beta: 1.073 Downside Beta: 0.977 Asymmetry: +0.096
📐
Composition Alignment
Nifty 500's mid/small weight has grown to ~29% — a hurdle the fund cannot match
Nifty 500 large-cap weight fell from 81% (2010) to 71% (2025). Growing mid/small exposure creates a structural benchmark drag for a large-cap dominated equity sleeve. Nifty 50's 100% large-cap is consistent and replicable.
N500 large-cap: 81% → 71% Small cap: 5.7% → 10.4% N50: 100% large-cap always
